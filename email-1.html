How Much Do You Really Know About CO2 Refrigeration?

Outdated facts and misinformation have left too many people thinking that CO2 systems are too complicated, too inefficient or too expensive. Here’s the truth: Common myths keep businesses like yours from harnessing the benefits of CO2. Let’s clear up some of the most frequent misconceptions.

CO2 systems use more energy than HFC systems.
Properly designed CO2 systems use a similar amount of energy as synthetic options in warm climates. Cool climate operation and enhancements like ThermoBoost, low-pressure injectors and parallel compression can make CO2 even more efficient.
 
Transcritical CO2 systems don’t work in warm climates.
Technology has advanced, and transcritical systems are practical and efficient in any climate, including warmer ambient temperatures. Today, there is no such thing as the “CO2 Equator.”
 
CO2 systems are too complex.
Modern innovations such as electronic expansion valves (EEV) and electronically controlled components have dramatically simplified CO2 system operations, saving even more energy and providing a future AI platform. While the engineering is advanced, it’s easier than ever to use CO2 systems.

What Are the Benefits of CO2 Refrigeration?

CO2 is more than just a “green” alternative. These systems offer advantages for businesses looking to control costs and boost their bottom line.

  • Lower Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
  • Lower service and maintenance costs
  • Lower refrigerant costs

With a Global Warming Potential (GWP) of 1, CO2 is a safe, reliable and sustainable refrigerant. As long as you’re working with experts who understand how to design, manufacture and install these systems properly, CO2 systems are efficient in all kinds of refrigeration applications.
